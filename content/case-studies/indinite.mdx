## Overview

Indinite needed a launch-ready identity and mobile shopping experience that could feel premium from day one without overcomplicating the purchase journey.

The work focused on aligning brand tone, visual language, and product interaction patterns so the first release felt coherent across every customer touchpoint.

## Challenge

The team was entering a crowded category where brand recall mattered almost as much as conversion. The early product also needed to balance editorial storytelling with a fast, reliable checkout flow on mobile.

### What shaped the direction

- First impressions had to communicate confidence before users learned the product story.
- The mobile journey needed to reduce friction without flattening the brand personality.
- The design system had to support rapid iteration after launch.

## Approach

The solution paired a more ownable visual identity with a clearer mobile hierarchy. Product discovery, item detail, and checkout states were simplified so the brand could stay expressive without getting in the way of decisions.

Typography, motion cues, and image pacing were tuned to make the product feel deliberate rather than generic, especially in high-intent shopping moments.
