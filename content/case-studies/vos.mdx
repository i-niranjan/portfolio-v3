## Overview

VOS was built around voice interaction, but the surrounding interface still had to carry context, state, and trust. The goal was to reduce friction for people who depended on accessibility-first interaction patterns without making the experience feel clinical or limited.

## Challenge

Voice products often break down when users lose visibility into system state. For VOS, that made accessibility a structural requirement rather than a feature layer.

### Design priorities

- Keep spoken and visual feedback in sync.
- Reduce ambiguity around listening, processing, and action states.
- Make advanced workflows feel possible without increasing cognitive load.

## Approach

The interface emphasized clear state changes, stronger hierarchy around feedback, and calmer visual pacing. The product had to support confidence at speed, especially for people using it as a power tool rather than a novelty.
